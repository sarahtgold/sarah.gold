Sarah is a designer interested in privacy, security and networks in the public domain. She creates interventions that show how technology can respect more of our rights.

Winning many awards including NESTA New Radical in 2016, Forbes 30 under 30 and a Creative Conscience Gold prize, she founded IF, where she works as CEO. IF is working with a variety of clients including DeepMind, Consumers International and the Co-op. Previously Sarah co-founded WikiHouse Foundation, a non-profit organisation.

Sarah sits on the board for Tech for Good, as a practitioner for the Research Institute in the Science of Cyber Security and is a Fellow of the RSA.

She has expertise in technology, data and networks in the public domain. Sarah recently spoke to a Parliamentary Select Committee, expressing her views on data sharing and accountability of machine learning.

Sarah is a member of the Robin Hood Co-operative, an activist hedge fund that invests in projects that protect and promote the commons.
